# AI R&D Multiplier

The **AI R&D Multiplier** (or Progress Multiplier) is a quantitative measure of how much an AI system accelerates the pace of AI research and development relative to human-only research.

---

## Definition

An AI R&D multiplier of **X** means that the research team makes as much algorithmic progress in 1 week *with* AI as they would in **X** weeks *without* it.

- **1.5x Multiplier**: Research is 50% faster (Early 2026 forecast).
- **10x Multiplier**: 1 year of progress happens every month (June 2027 forecast).
- **50x Multiplier**: 1 year of progress happens every week (Late 2027 forecast).

---

## Component Focus: Algorithmic Progress

The multiplier specifically refers to **Improved Algorithms** (better training methods, architecture shifts, data filtering). It does *not* refer to hardware scaling (compute), though it is often bottlenecked by it.

- **Human Base**: Historically, algorithmic progress has halved the cost of training a specific class of model (like GPT-4) every year.
- **AI Acceleration**: With a high multiplier, this "halving time" can shrink to weeks or days.

---

## Why It Matters

This metric predicts the "slope" of the **Intelligence Explosion**. In [[Source: AI 2027]], once the multiplier hits 10x, the lead of a company like [[OpenBrain]] becomes insurmountable by humans alone.

## Open Questions

- **Diminishing Returns**: At what point does better software hit physical or theoretical limits, regardless of the multiplier?
- **Compute Bottlenecks**: If software gets 100x better, but compute takes months to build, can the multiplier be fully utilized?
